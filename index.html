Phishing Protection for Seniors: Stay Safe Online

A straightforward guide to recognizing and avoiding phishing scams

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a scam where cybercriminals pretend to be trustworthy (like your bank or a friend) to trick you into giving sensitive information, such as passwords or credit card numbers. It's like a fishing hook trying to catch you!

Common Signs of a Phishing Attempt

Unexpected Emails or Calls: If someone contacts you out of the blue asking for personal info, be wary.
Urgent Language: Phrases like "Act now or your account will be suspended!" create panic to rush you.
Suspicious Links: Hover over links (don't click) to see if the URL looks real (e.g., bankofamerica.com vs. bankofamerica-login.net).
Typos or Poor Grammar: Legitimate companies proofread; scammers often don't.
Requests for Money or Info: Real organizations won't ask for payments via gift cards or wire transfers.

Top Tips to Protect Yourself

  • Verify the Source: Contact the company directly using a known phone number or website, not the one provided in the suspicious message.
  • Use Strong Passwords: Make them unique and long. Consider a password manager.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): This adds an extra layer of security to your accounts.
  • Keep Software Updated: Updates often include security patches against scams.
  • Don't Click Unknown Links: Type the URL yourself or use bookmarks.
  • Report Suspicious Activity: Forward phishing emails to reportphishing@apwg.org or call the FTC at 1-877-FTC-HELP.
  • Educate Family: Share this guide with loved ones and discuss online safety regularly.
