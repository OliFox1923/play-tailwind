What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where scammers impersonate legitimate organizations (like banks, retailers, or government agencies) to trick you into sharing sensitive information, such as passwords, credit card details, or Social Security numbers. These scams often come through emails, text messages, or phone calls.

Think of it like a fishing hook: Scammers "bait" you with a fake message to "catch" your personal information.

Common Signs of a Phishing Attempt

Unexpected Contact: Emails, texts, or calls from unknown sources asking for personal details.
Urgent or Threatening Language: Messages like "Your account will be locked!" or "Act now!" to pressure you.
Suspicious Links or Attachments: Hover over links (don’t click!) to check if the URL looks legitimate.
Poor Grammar or Typos: Legitimate organizations rarely make spelling mistakes.

Top Tips to Protect Yourself

  • Verify the Source: Contact the organization directly using a trusted phone number or website.
  • Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Combine letters, numbers, and symbols. Consider a password manager.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adds an extra security step, like a code sent to your phone.
  • Keep Software Updated: Install updates for your devices and apps to patch security vulnerabilities.
  • Avoid Unknown Links: Type URLs manually or use bookmarks for trusted sites.
  • Report Scams: Forward phishing emails to reportphishing@apwg.org or contact the FTC at 1-877-FTC-HELP.
